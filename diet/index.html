Here’s how the gut and brain interact with each other:

Your Gut

  • Produces key neurotransmitters like serotonin (90–95%), GABA, and dopamine.
  • Supports BDNF expression when nourished by fiber and polyphenol-rich diets.
  • Maintains immune balance; dysbiosis triggers pro-inflammatory cytokines.
  • Breaks down food into vital micronutrients (e.g., B12, folate, magnesium).
  • Influences the stress response via the HPA axis.

Your Brain

  • Uses neurotransmitters to regulate mood, sleep, and cognition.
  • BDNF supports learning, memory, and long-term brain health.
  • Inflammation in the brain raises risk of cognitive decline and Alzheimer’s.
  • Relies on gut-derived nutrients for energy metabolism and resilience.
  • Lower cortisol release improves mood and buffers against stress.
