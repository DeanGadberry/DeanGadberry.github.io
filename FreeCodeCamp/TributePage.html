Euclid

The Father of Geometry

Euclid's published works still survive today.

Elements - an amalgamation of mathematical results that originated with earlier mathmeticians, compiled in one volume for ease of reference.

Data - deals with the nature and implications of "given" information in geometrical problems.

On Divisions of Figures - the only surviving Arabic translation concerns the divions of geometrical figures into two or more equal parts or into parts in given ratios.

Catortrics - written regarding the mathematical theory of mirrors.

Phaenomena - a treatise on spherical astronomy

Optics - the earliest surviving Greek treatise on perspective. Euclid expounds on emission theory, which suggest that discrete rays emenate from the eye, resulting in sight.

Some of his published works are lost

Conics - a work on conic sections later extended by Apollonius of Perga.

Porisms - an outgrowth of work with conic sections. The exact meaning of the title is controversial.

Pseudaria or Book of Fallacies - a simple text regarding errors in reasoning.

Surface Loci - concerned with sets of points on surfaces or sets of points which were surfaces. The work may have dealt with quadric surfaces.

On the Heavy and the Light - a work on mechanics attributed to Euclid by Arabic sources.

On the Balance - considers deifinitions, axioms and propositions regarding the lever.
